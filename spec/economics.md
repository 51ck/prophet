# Economics

## Intent

Keep the service sustainable while delivering the locked job: esoteric answers when ordinary routes fail — without wasting the seeker’s time or money. Authenticity and ritual quality come first; monetization must not corrupt deck honesty or pressure fake “good” cards.

Value to the seeker: fewer empty sessions, no invented cards, bounded arc — paid access (when it arrives) should still feel cheaper in time and trust than hollow alternatives.

## Constraints

- Budget is limited — uncontrolled free usage is a risk to survival
- Payment, if any, should fit Telegram-native habits where possible
- Pricing and limits must stay understandable to the seeker

## Deferred direction

Likely path: **Telegram Stars** (or equivalent native paid access) after the reading feel is proven.

This doc does **not** define:

- Prices, packs, or free tiers
- Paywalls per shuffle vs per session
- Refunds, gifts, or tips UX

Those belong to a later product phase. See [roadmap.md](roadmap.md).

## Principles for when monetization arrives

- Pay for access or capacity — never for a “better” card outcome
- Ritual authenticity unchanged for paying and non-paying seekers within an allowed session
- Stewardship precedes full paid launch: manifest, public website (terms + description), usage statistics, admin panel for tracking, plus limits/abuse control — see Phase 4 in [roadmap.md](roadmap.md)
